I Love Steak You Should Learn to Cook it!

Make sure to look at the video to follow visually

  1. Place a wire rack on top of a sheet tray. Add the steak to the wire rack and season liberally with salt on all sides. Place the seasoned steak in the refrigerator and leave it uncovered overnight.
    • 1 ribeye
    • Kosher salt to taste
  2. Preheat the oven to 300°F (149°C). Insert a probe thermometer into the thickest part of the steak and set it to 115°F (46°C).

  3. Transfer the steak to the oven and cook for 35–50 minutes, depending on thickness. Once the internal temperature reaches 115°F (46°C), remove it from the oven and set aside.

  4. Preheat a 12-inch stainless steel skillet over medium-high heat. Brush the neutral oil all over the steak and lightly season with salt.
    • 1 tablespoon neutral oil

  5. Add the steak to the hot skillet, followed by the butter, garlic (cut side down), rosemary, sage, and thyme.
    • ½ stick salted butter
    • 1 garlic head
    • 2 large sprigs rosemary
    • 2 large sprigs sage
    • 6 sprigs thyme

  6. As the butter melts, tip the pan toward you and continuously baste the steak. Sear for 1–2 minutes until a deep crust forms.

  7. Flip the steak and repeat basting for another 1–2 minutes. Remove the steak from the heat and transfer to a cutting board.

  8. Rest for 5–10 minutes, then slice the steak into ½-inch thick pieces, cutting against the grain.

  9. Transfer to a serving platter, top with a few slices of compound butter, and serve warm.
